# LinkedIn `Cmd+Enter` → Submit Comment

> A Chrome extension for people who have **absolutely zero milliseconds** to spare on moving a finger to the trackpad and want to submit LinkedIn comments with a keyboard shortcut instead (`Cmd+Enter` on Mac, `Ctrl+Enter` on Windows/Linux).

### but why?!

Look. I get it. You're a busy person. You've got things to do, places to be, thoughts to share on LinkedIn. You type out a beautifully crafted comment, something witty, something insightful, maybe even something that'll get you 47 reactions and a connection request from a VP you've never met.

And then.

You have to **move your finger to the trackpad**.

Drag it allllll the way over to that little blue "Post" button.

*Click.*

The audacity. The sheer, unmitigated **waste of human potential**. Scientists estimate that the average knowledge worker loses up to 0.8 seconds per LinkedIn comment click. That's 0.8 seconds you could've spent... typing the next LinkedIn comment.

**This ends today.**

## What It Does

Lets you hit `Cmd+Enter` (Mac) or `Ctrl+Enter` (Win/Linux) to submit a LinkedIn comment.

That's it. That's the whole thing. You're welcome.

---

## Install

1. Clone or download this repo
2. Open Chrome → `chrome://extensions`
3. Enable **Developer Mode** (top right toggle)
4. Click **Load unpacked** → select this folder
5. Go to LinkedIn. Type a comment. Hit `Cmd+Enter`. Feel the freedom.

---

## How It Works

A tiny content script listens for `Cmd+Enter` / `Ctrl+Enter` while you're in a LinkedIn comment box, then programmatically clicks the submit button for you.

No background scripts. No network calls. No data collection. No AI. No blockchain. No synergy. Just a keyboard shortcut doing exactly one job.

---

## Why Does This Exist

Because one day, mid-comment, the author looked at the trackpad and thought:

> *"I am a software engineer. I have the power to make computers do things. Why am I the one doing the clicking?"*

And thus, 20 minutes of extension writing saved an estimated 0.8 seconds per comment for the rest of eternity. The math works out. Trust the process.
